# 002 - Independent Store And Thrift CD Finds

Source: user-provided article about recent finds from independent music stores,
thrift stores, and cheap used CDs.

## Digging Journeys

- Not much happening in new releases -> goes to independent stores, thrifts, and
  similar places -> cheap CD glut plus donated collections create the chance of
  overlooked finds -> hunts for interesting discs without paying full price.

- Likes thrift stores because donation boxes can hide family collections nobody
  understood -> checks shelves often -> low prices make the hunt worth repeated
  visits -> surprise becomes part of the value.

- Huge fan of The Church's 1985-1995 period -> keeps seeing "Magician Among the
  Spirits" at St. Vincent DePaul -> cracked case, 1996 date, and $2 price keep
  him from buying -> 50 percent off sign lowers the risk -> buys it -> learns it
  has a strange failed-distribution history -> treats it as essential for fans
  of that era.

- Buys the Church CD -> replaces the broken case -> inspects disc and booklet ->
  notices it was sourced from Australia -> rips it -> metadata appears -> checks
  Discogs, Amazon, eBay, Tidal, and Qobuz -> learns the physical disc is scarce
  while streaming is easy.

- Longtime Bill Bruford fan -> missed the Bruford solo period in real time ->
  finds "Master Strokes 1978-1985" at 2nd and Charles for $4 -> buys it as an
  entry point into the gap -> follows the compilation into Bruford albums,
  Bruford/Moraz records, vinyl, and related prog history.

- Knows Deep Purple's "Machine Head" deeply -> standard catalog CD sounds
  congested compared with vinyl -> shops CD Warehouse for catalog discs around
  $5 -> finds a 2012 remaster/Japanese CD -> buys for the sound upgrade -> hears
  more power, dynamics, and clarity -> old favorite feels new again.

- First hears Cyrus Chestnut by winning "Revelation" from a college jazz station
  promo -> the disc becomes a system-test favorite -> spends years unable to
  find more Cyrus Chestnut CDs easily -> visits a weak Goodwill shelf in
  Charleston -> scans the small stock several times -> "Soul Food" jumps out by
  artist recognition -> buys it -> sound quality and personnel make it a keeper.

## What Moves The Choice

- Price: $1 or less is low-risk; $2 requires selectivity; $4-$5 needs a known
  artist, catalog gap, upgrade, or other strong reason; a discount can flip the
  decision.
- Known anchors: The Church, Bill Bruford, Deep Purple, Cyrus Chestnut.
- Gaps: missed band periods, side projects, rare releases, better masters,
  related players.
- Friction: cracked case, wrong era, poor shelf, higher price, unknown album.
- Confirmation: inspect booklet/disc, rip to server, metadata lookup, Discogs,
  Amazon, eBay, Tidal, Qobuz, Spotify.
- Payoff: cheap find, rare story, better sound, a path into related records.
