# 003 - Kathy Iandoli, The Lost Art Of Cratedigging

Source: Kathy Iandoli, "The Lost Art Of Cratedigging", Cuepoint/Medium,
2014-09-24. Source URL: https://medium.com/cuepoint/the-lost-art-of-cratedigging-4ed652643618

## Digging Journeys

- Teenager looking through basement boxes for something unrelated -> finds her
  mother's stack of old 45s -> recognizes some names and discovers others from
  an era she missed -> the physical act of unearthing records sticks -> later
  becomes a record collector.

- Childhood 45 discovery -> tastes shift toward hip-hop -> realizes hip-hop was
  built from pieces of old records -> old vinyl becomes source material, not
  just old music -> starts cratedigging in college.

- College breaks -> leaves campus for downtown Bloomfield, New Jersey -> checks
  Crazy Rhythms, book-store cellars, instrument repair shops with random piles,
  and church swapmeets -> digs despite dust allergy and male-dominated rooms ->
  builds a collection through hidden physical spots.

- Loves a popular song and wants its source DNA -> hears Madonna's "Hung Up" and
  recognizes the ABBA sample -> chases the original single configuration ->
  searches a Brooklyn basement -> finds ABBA's "The King Has Lost His Crown"
  because "Gimme! Gimme! Gimme!" is the B-side.

- Wants a record collection before playback matters -> starts cratedigging years
  before owning a turntable -> acquisition and discovery come first -> hearing
  the finds is secondary at that stage.

- Hip-hop producer needs a breakbeat or useful sound snippet -> hunts record
  stores and early-morning record fairs -> finds vinyl -> plays it on a
  turntable -> records the chosen piece into a sampler/drum machine -> layers it
  into a beat.

- Golden Age producers need raw material before other diggers clear the good
  stock -> arrive early at Roosevelt Hotel Record Convention -> peel through
  crates under one roof with other producers -> search feels like sport,
  competition, and community at once.

- DJ Premier digs for samples -> reads producer, label, and instrumental credits
  -> if one contributor made something great, follows their other work -> credits
  become a trail to the next record.

- Younger/digital producer needs sounds quickly -> uses MP3s, YouTube,
  WhoSampled, Shazam, and software instead of dusty-store hunting -> skims
  albums for usable sounds -> can make records without deep catalog knowledge.

- Parent wants to introduce a child to music basics -> buys a small turntable
  and Stevie Wonder 45s -> child can handle vinyl and remember songs by label
  color before reading -> tactile memory becomes a gateway into music.

## What Moves The Choice

- Trigger: accident, sample recognition, beatmaking need, collection-building,
  wanting the source behind a song.
- Places: basement boxes, downtown Bloomfield shops, secret cellars, repair
  shops, church swapmeets, Brooklyn basement, Roosevelt Hotel convention.
- Cues: sample, B-side, label, producer, instrumental credits, contributor
  names, label color.
- Pressure: early arrival before records are picked over, competition with other
  producers, dusty physical conditions, male-dominated rooms.
- Tools: turntable, sampler, drum machine, liner notes, Numark, MP3s, YouTube,
  WhoSampled, Shazam.
- Split: vinyl digging builds context and lineage; digital digging can find a
  usable sound faster with less attachment to the record.
