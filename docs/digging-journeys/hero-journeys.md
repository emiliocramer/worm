# Hero Journey Seeds

Derived from entries 001-007.

These are not source entries. They are possible routes Worm can choose before it
looks for a real, brand-new, catalog-verified song.

These routes depend on secondary effect nodes. See
`docs/secondary-effect-nodes.md`.

Shape:

`brain signal -> dig through this path -> candidate shape -> source trail`

## 1. The Closed Door Artist

Brain signal: the user has weak/negative/no history with a popular artist, but
likes a producer, side player, label, scene, or adjacent sound around them.

Dig route: skip the obvious artist hits. Follow producer credits, side projects,
backing players, collaborators, and later offshoots.

Candidate shape: a track from the side project or collaborator network that
makes the dismissed artist make sense from a safer angle.

Source trail: 006 The Black Keys -> Danger Mouse -> Dan Auerbach -> The Arcs;
003 DJ Premier following producer/label/player credits.

## 2. The Missed Chapter

Brain signal: the user loves an artist or scene, but their listening clusters
around one "classic" period and ignores nearby years, late albums, side projects,
solo work, live records, or remasters.

Dig route: find the gap just outside their known period. Look for the weird late
record, failed-release story, compilation entry point, or improved master.

Candidate shape: a non-obvious track that belongs to a known obsession but comes
from a neglected chapter.

Source trail: 002 The Church's post-classic-period "Magician Among the Spirits";
002 Bruford solo-period gap; 006 The Arcs after Dan Auerbach.

## 3. Source DNA

Brain signal: the user likes sample-based music, hip-hop, production videos,
covers, breaks, remixes, or songs with obvious lineage.

Dig route: move backward from the song they know into samples, B-sides,
breakbeats, original recordings, session players, and labels.

Candidate shape: an older source record that explains something they already
love without sounding like a normal recommendation.

Source trail: 003 Madonna -> ABBA B-side chase; 003 breakbeat hunting; 005 "The
Clapping Song" becoming a hip-hop break; 007 sampling records for fragments.

## 4. The Context Flip

Brain signal: the user likes a scene that repurposes older music: hip-hop,
dance, soul nights, DJ mixes, edits, covers, sync, or genre-crossing playlists.

Dig route: find a track that was one thing in its original era and became
another thing in a new room, country, dance floor, or production context.

Candidate shape: not necessarily rare, but newly useful when framed through the
user's actual scene.

Source trail: 005 Northern Soul importing U.S. soul into a UK scene; 005 1960s
pop becoming a hip-hop break; 001 old records becoming present-tense medicine.

## 5. One Record For Right Now

Brain signal: the user is in a state: blocked, overloaded, restless, late-night,
working, traveling, commuting, or returning to a routine.

Dig route: search by desired effect before genre. Look for music that is both a
departure and a regulator: faraway, old, warm, loose, meditative, live, or
textural.

Candidate shape: one specific track that fits the moment, not a playlist.

Source trail: 001 writer's block -> Head Sounds -> Pharoah Sanders; 006 live
Redding as imperfect human energy; 007 tactile sampling as a way out of sterile
production.

## 6. The Cheap-Risk Bin

Brain signal: the user likes low-popularity music, older formats, thrift/used
media, catalog oddities, long-tail albums, or has patience for imperfect finds.

Dig route: simulate the bargain bin. Search low-cost/risk-equivalent zones:
overstock jazz, obscure compilations, poor-selling albums, odd labels, old live
sets, rough recordings, forgotten soundtracks.

Candidate shape: a track that feels found because it comes from an ignored pile,
not an obvious recommendation graph.

Source trail: 002 thrift CD thresholds; 004 dollar records and eMusic credit
math; 007 dirt-cheap sample LPs.

## 7. The Local Oddity

Brain signal: the brain has place anchors: hometown, travel, local scenes,
photos, calendar locations, regional YouTube interests, or stores/venues the
user mentions.

Dig route: search from place outward: regional labels, local scenes, foreign
shops, city-specific recordings, touring scenes, or records moved from one
country into another.

Candidate shape: a song that feels like it came from somewhere, not just from a
genre cluster.

Source trail: 001 Brooklyn stores and Landover family memory; 005 U.S. soul into
Northern Soul; 007 Korea record-store oddities; 004 West Eighth Street jazz
overstock.

## 8. The Ignored Online Crate

Brain signal: the user already lives in digital discovery spaces: YouTube,
Bandcamp, blogs, niche creators, production channels, indie catalogs, or deep
search habits.

Dig route: treat the internet like a messy shop, not a recommendation feed.
Search low-attention pages, tiny labels, odd distributors, one-off producers,
old uploads, and catalog corners with weak metadata.

Candidate shape: a real track with low obviousness that feels under-noticed, not
just obscure for sport.

Source trail: 001 Bandcamp for global digging; 004 eMusic shrinking catalog
gems; 005 Bandcamp as modern rarity; 003 digital digging as a faster but thinner
route.

## 9. The Live Room

Brain signal: the user likes musicianship, old soul/jazz/rock, live albums,
performance videos, concerts, instrumental detail, or rougher recordings.

Dig route: search for live versions, radio sessions, archival concert sets,
alternate takes, and recordings where the band breathes.

Candidate shape: a live recording where imperfection, ad libs, tempo, crowd, or
player restraint is the reason it lands.

Source trail: 006 Otis Redding at the Whisky A Go Go; 002 classic-rock concert
sets; 001 Pharoah Sanders live album.

## 10. The Texture Route

Brain signal: the user responds to analog warmth, vinyl, old recordings,
remasters, production gear, sampling, lo-fi texture, acoustic drums, or sound
quality language.

Dig route: search by recording character: crackle, room, tape, sampler color,
acoustic drums, worn vinyl, remaster clarity, rough production, or speed/pitch
transformation.

Candidate shape: a track where the sound object matters as much as composition.

Source trail: 001 ringwear/crackles/pops; 002 Machine Head remaster and Bruford
acoustic drums; 006 live imperfections; 007 sampler/turntable texture.

## 11. The Album-First Dig

Brain signal: the user saves albums, plays long tracks, avoids singles, follows
album eras, or builds playlists around records rather than tracks.

Dig route: find the album path first, then choose the song. Favor deep cuts,
long-form jazz, side-two moments, title tracks, and records with strong context.

Candidate shape: a song that acts as an entrance to a full album.

Source trail: 004 album-first eMusic buying; 001 liner notes and jackets; 002
compilations as entry points; 007 full passages as sample sources.

## 12. The Human Curator Thread

Brain signal: the user has trusted taste people: family, friends, DJs, writers,
labels, YouTube creators, bandmates, store employees, or scenes they orbit.

Dig route: follow the person, not the genre. What did that person introduce,
play, produce, write about, stock, sample, or bring back from somewhere else?

Candidate shape: a track that feels passed along by a real human chain.

Source trail: 001 cousin Eric and family listening; 003 DJ/producers at Fat Beats
and Roosevelt Hotel; 005 knowledgeable dealers and DJs; 006 musician memory.

## How Worm Should Use These

- Pick a route before picking a song.
- Build or retrieve real candidates inside that route.
- Reject known artists, known tracks, obvious hits, and normal-platform adjacent
  picks.
- Catalog-verify the surviving candidates.
- Explain the winner through the route, not through generic similarity.
