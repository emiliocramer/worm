# 011 — The Version Chain (Real Rock riddim)

Real digging story: one rhythm, hundreds of records.

In 1967 at Studio One on Brentford Road, Kingston, the Sound Dimension —
Jackie Mittoo's three-note organ figure over Boris Gardiner's bass — cut a
short instrumental called "Real Rock" for Coxsone Dodd. It became the most
versioned riddim in reggae history: over a hundred documented versions and
recuts across reggae, dancehall, and dub, plus 100+ samples in hip-hop, jungle,
and drum and bass, and countless sound-system dubplates. A reggae fan who
loves one tune on Real Rock has a lifetime of digging inside the same three
notes — Willie Williams' "Armagideon Time" is the same riddim wearing a
different song.

Journey shape:

```text
love for a reggae/dub/dancehall tune
-> the riddim underneath it
-> every other artist who voiced that riddim across decades
-> a version chain where each record recontextualizes the last
```
