# 015 — The Interpretation Chain (Gould's two Goldbergs)

Real digging story: in classical music the artist axis inverts — the work
stays fixed and the performer is the discovery.

Glenn Gould recorded Bach's Goldberg Variations twice: the 1955 debut, brash
and breezy, a young virtuoso proving himself; and the 1981 revisit, slower and
inward, a unified reading by a man who had lived with the piece's
contradictions for decades. Listeners argue the pair endlessly — same notes,
different lifetimes. A classical listener digs by walking the interpretation
chain: the same work under different hands, tempos, and rooms, where each
recording recontextualizes the others.

Journey shape:

```text
classical taste (a loved work or composer)
-> the same work recorded by different performers across eras
-> the reading that disagrees with the famous one
-> a recording that makes a known piece brand new
```
