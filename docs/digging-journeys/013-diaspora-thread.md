# 013 — The Diaspora Thread (cumbia rebajada)

Real digging story: a heritage lineage becomes its own crate.

Colombian migrants brought cumbia and vallenato records to Monterrey's Colonia
Independencia, where neighborhood sonideros played them at family parties. In
the early 1990s sonidero Gabriel Dueñez's player broke — the motor overheated
and dragged the record slow — and the crowd asked for more of the "rebajada"
sound: voices deepened, accordions stretched. Dueñez pressed the first
rebajada cassette in 1992 and sold slowed cumbias on the Puente del Papa; an
accidental genre grew out of a migration story. Digging a diaspora thread
means following the music of a family lineage — the parents' radio canon, the
records that crossed a border, and the scenes that reworked them — rather
than a genre chart.

Journey shape:

```text
heritage signal (childhood-lineage music named in the profile, heritage

-> a record that sounds like home and brand new at once
```
