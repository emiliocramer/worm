# 014 — The Split & Demo (hardcore DIY infrastructure)

Real digging story: in DIY scenes the format IS the discovery mechanism.

Split 7-inches were "the lingua franca of the DIY scene" — two bands splitting
pressing costs to cement a friendship or link scenes separated by distance.
You bought the split for the band you knew and left with the band you didn't.
Demo tapes moved the same way: reviewed in cut-and-paste zines, traded by
mail, sometimes reaching a label without the band's knowledge — The Splits'
recordings traveled to P Trash and Dirtnap Records that way and came back as
LP offers. Bandcamp is the modern shape of the same infrastructure: splits,
demos, and EPs on tiny label pages, where a hardcore fan digs by release
format, not by artist search.

Journey shape:

```text
DIY-scene taste (hardcore, punk, metalcore, screamo, grind)
-> the format infrastructure: splits, demos, EPs, comps, label pages
-> the other side of the split, the demo that predates the album
-> a band discovered the way the scene actually spreads
```
