# 001 - Marcus J. Moore, Crate Digging

Source: Marcus J. Moore, "A Music Obsession That's Adventurous and Soothing",
The New York Times, 2021-09-24.

## Digging Journeys

- Writer's block in a Brooklyn apartment -> needs air and music from somewhere
  else -> walks to Head Sounds Records -> goes to jazz -> spots Pharoah Sanders'
  "Live at the East" -> buys it before another digger can -> "Healing Song"
  becomes the balm that gets him unstuck.

- Wants music from a faraway place/time -> looks for underground spiritual jazz,
  Afro-disco, ringwear, crackles, pops -> trusts old records that people return
  to decades later -> lands on a 1972 Pharoah Sanders live album -> gets both
  adventure and calm.

- Grows up in a house full of pop, funk, soul, gospel, go-go, rap, R&B, Bowie,
  Madonna -> cousin Eric the DJ brings home early word on MC Hammer and Common
  Sense -> learns that radio is only one layer -> carries that instinct into
  music journalism, curation, and record digging.

- Before living in New York -> takes buses into the city to look for records ->
  finds fewer shops because streaming is eating the market -> record stores
  still work as community hubs and discovery rooms -> vinyl's comeback keeps the
  physical path alive.

- Uses Bandcamp for online digging -> finds paths like South African boogie and
  forgotten ambient -> still prefers stores for the record he did not know he
  needed until the cover was in front of him -> physical context changes what
  becomes findable.

- Enters a shop where staff mostly leave him alone -> dives into sections ->
  flips jackets -> samples on the turntable if needed -> follows gut -> chooses
  from the stack instead of from a search box.

- Sees a record with scarcity/status -> wants to be the one who found and talks
  about it -> buying becomes sharing -> sharing becomes preservation and paying
  forward what family taught him.

## What Moves The Choice

- State: blocked, heavy, restless, needing air.
- Pull: old records, other eras, other places, underground or less-heralded
  artists.
- Cues: jacket, ringwear, crackle, pop, dust, label, release year, liner notes.
- People: family, DJ cousin, store employees, other diggers.
- Method: section browsing, flipping, sampling, trusting gut.
- Payoff: one record that fits the moment, gives him something to share, and
  connects present taste to old memory.
